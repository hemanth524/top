NATIONAL INSTITUTE OF TECHNOLOGY
WARANGAL

The foundation stone for the college was laid by Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru on 10 October 1959 in Kazipet. The classes for the first batch of students in Civil, Electrical and Mechanical Engineering branches commenced on 12 September 1959 in temporary sheds erected in the Balasamudram area of Hanamkonda. Later temporary sheds were also erected in the Industrial Colony of Warangal for conducting lecture classes. Laboratory classes and workshop classes were conducted in the Government Polytechnic, Warangal in the initial stages. The conduction of classes on the premises of the permanent site in Kazipet commenced in January 1963.

The bachelor's programme in Chemical Engineering was initiated in 1964.[6] The following year saw the start of the Metallurgical Engineering programme. In 1971, the college started the undergraduate program in Electronics and Communication Engineering. BTech in Computer Science & Engineering was started in 1983 and MTech in Computer Science & Engineering in 1987.[7] In 2006, the institute started a bachelor's program in Biotechnology

India's former Prime Minister Pandit Jawaharlal Nehru founded the National Institute of Technology at Warangal in 1959. Previously, NIT Warangal was known as Regional Engineering College. NIT Warangal has been ranked 21 and 45 under the ‘Engineering’ and 'Overall' categories by the NIRF 2022 Rankings. It is the first among the 30 NITs established in the country. NIT Warangal currently has 13 academic departments and a few advanced research centres in various disciplines of Engineering. NIT Warangal offers 8 UG programmes in different disciplines of Engineering, and 29 PG programmes in Business Administration, Computer Application, Sciences, and Engineering. Additionally, the institute also offers a PhD programme in Management, Engineering, Humanities, Sciences and Physical Education.
